About Me

I have a B.Sc. in Psychology and I am currently pursuing a master's degree in Methodology and Statistics with a focus on the behavioral and social sciences. In my master's thesis, I am exploring Bayesian Evidence Synthesis, which involves aggregating evidence across conceptual replications to assess the veracity and generalizability of theories. While I have a keen interest in statistical research and computational methods, I am also passionate about global societal challenges and the potential of interdisciplinary research to address them. To further broaden my knowledge, I am participating in Graduate Honours Interdisciplinary Seminars that allow me to explore various disciplines beyond my field of expertise. In addition to my research work, I would be interested in facilitating the communication and the application of scientific findings outside of academia.
